GRANT AGREEMENT
Community Health Access Program
Award Number: CDPH-2026-CHAP-0042

PARTIES
Grantor: California Department of Public Health (CDPH)
Grantee: CareBridge Health
Grant Period: January 1, 2026 – December 31, 2026
Total Award Amount: $250,000

---

SECTION 1: PURPOSE

This grant supports the expansion of primary care access services for underserved Medicaid-eligible populations in Los Angeles County. Funds shall be used exclusively for personnel, direct service delivery, and community outreach activities as described in the approved work plan.

---

SECTION 2: REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Quarterly Financial Reports
The grantee shall submit a quarterly financial report no later than 30 days after the end of each calendar quarter. Reports are due on the following dates:
  - Q1 Report: April 30, 2026
  - Q2 Report: July 31, 2026
  - Q3 Report: October 31, 2026
  - Q4 / Final Report: January 31, 2027

Each report must include a budget variance analysis, a breakdown of expenditures by category, and copies of invoices for any single expenditure exceeding $5,000.

2.2 Quarterly Progress Reports
Concurrent with each financial report, the grantee must submit a program progress narrative covering:
  (a) Number of beneficiaries served by service type
  (b) Status of activities against the approved work plan
  (c) Barriers encountered and corrective actions taken

2.3 Annual Beneficiary Impact Assessment
By December 15, 2026, the grantee must submit a comprehensive impact assessment including:
  - Total unduplicated beneficiaries served
  - Health outcomes data (A1C rates, blood pressure control, vaccination rates)
  - Patient satisfaction survey results (minimum 10% response rate)
  - Community health worker caseload analysis

---

SECTION 3: FINANCIAL COMPLIANCE

3.1 Allowable Costs
All expenditures must comply with 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance). Unallowable costs include alcohol, lobbying, entertainment, and fines or penalties.

3.2 Budget Modifications
Any reallocation of funds between budget categories exceeding 10% of the total award or $10,000 (whichever is less) requires prior written approval from CDPH. Requests must be submitted at least 30 days in advance.

3.3 Audit Requirements
If total federal expenditures exceed $750,000 in a fiscal year, the grantee must obtain a Single Audit in accordance with 2 CFR Part 200, Subpart F. The audit report must be submitted to CDPH within 9 months of the grantee's fiscal year end (due September 30, 2026).

3.4 Record Retention
All financial records, supporting documents, and programmatic records must be retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of final payment.

---

SECTION 4: SITE VISITS AND MONITORING

4.1 Scheduled Site Visit
CDPH reserves the right to conduct at least one announced monitoring site visit during the grant period. The grantee will receive a minimum of 14 days' notice. The first scheduled site visit is planned for June 15, 2026.

4.2 Desk Reviews
CDPH may conduct desk reviews of submitted reports at any time without prior notice.

---

SECTION 5: PROGRAMMATIC REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Staffing
All personnel charged to this grant must be employed by the grantee organization. Any change in key personnel (Program Director, Finance Lead) must be reported to CDPH within 10 business days.

5.2 Subcontracts
Any subcontract exceeding $10,000 must receive prior written approval from CDPH. Subcontractors are subject to the same compliance requirements as the grantee.

5.3 Data Privacy
All beneficiary data must be handled in compliance with HIPAA and California's Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA). A data security incident must be reported to CDPH within 48 hours of discovery.

---

SECTION 6: CLOSEOUT

Upon completion of the grant period, the grantee must submit all outstanding reports and return any unspent funds within 90 days. A formal grant closeout letter will be issued by CDPH upon satisfactory completion of all requirements.
